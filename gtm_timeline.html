Week 01
Jul 14–20
Surface the pain: no product, just the question
Products in focusNone. This week is the problem only.
▶ YouTube
Sharan appears on a podcast with Nikhil Kamath or Tanmay Bhat — a cultural voice, not a financial one. The conversation is about life, not investing. The moment that matters: “You’ve spent 3 years teaching people about money. What do 99% of financial influencers get wrong?” The problem is planted without naming a product.
◆ Instagram
Street interview reel — “If you had ₹1 crore, would you still go to work tomorrow?” Answers are unscripted. Some laugh. Some go quiet. A story poll runs alongside. The reel travels.
in LinkedIn + X
The podcast clip gets paid amplification targeted at senior professionals and founders — exactly the ICP. Organic posts: “Just got off Sharan’s latest podcast — game changer.”
◙ OOH + PR
Large format OOH across Bengaluru, Mumbai, Delhi. No logo. No product. Just the question: “If you had ₹1 crore, would you still go to work tomorrow?” People photograph it. The question does the work.
Week 02
Jul 21–27
The question keeps travelling — one post opens the waitlist
Products in focusBeta access opens quietly. Nothing named publicly.
▶ YouTube
No new video. The podcast clip continues to travel. Organic reach does the heavy lifting — no spend needed yet.
◆ Instagram
Sharan puts up a single post: “We’ve built something. I need 500 people to try it before we launch. Drop your name in the comments.” No other detail. Comments fill within hours.
in LinkedIn + X
Same post mirrored on LinkedIn. Redirects to Instagram for the beta drop. People who saw the OOH start connecting the dots.
◙ OOH + PR
The OOH stays. Same creative. Awareness is compounding.
Week 03
Jul 28–Aug 3
Beta users speak: the product earns its first word-of-mouth
Products in focusMembership + Baskets hinted through beta outcomes. Never named directly.
▶ YouTube
No new video. The podcast clip is still being amplified. Beta user outcomes are the story this week — not a campaign.
◆ Instagram
Beta users post on stories. Reshared by 1% Club. “The level of personalisation. Feels criminal.” No scripted brief. Authentic reactions only.
in LinkedIn + X
Same beta sentiment on LinkedIn. “The level of personalisation. Feels criminal.” FinTwit and professional networks start paying attention.
◙ OOH + PR
ET or TOI runs a piece: “SEBI is cleaning up India’s financial advice market.” No mention of 1% Club. The timing creates a backdrop of urgency — the editorial does the positioning work for free.
Week 04
Aug 4–10
One feature revealed — the AI Assistant becomes the entry point
Products in focusAI Assistant revealed. Everything else stays hidden.
▶ YouTube
Sharan posts one video: “I’m going to show you one feature. Just one.” He opens the AI Assistant live. The app answers with a specific, personalised plan. He says nothing else. Comments go crazy. The audience goes from curious to impatient.
◆ Instagram
A teaser reel — no voiceover, just the app screen and the answer. A countdown to Aug 15 appears in the story. The visual simplicity lets the product speak.
in LinkedIn + X
Nikhil Kamath posts: “Here’s why I chose to back 1% Club over 50 other fintech startups.” FinTwit starts paying serious attention. Other finance creators post organically.
◙ OOH + PR
No new OOH. The existing creative is still running. Adding anything now would dilute the reveal moment on Aug 15.
Week 05
Aug 11–17
The app goes live.
Products in focusMembership · Baskets · AI Assistant · Money School · FIRE number as the entry hook
▶ YouTube
The full launch video goes live on Independence Day. Sharan opens with: “Do you know your FIRE number?” He types it into the app live. Then walks through Baskets and Money School. Not a feature list — a story of financial clarity.
◆ Instagram
The launch reel goes up simultaneously. Early access at Founder Price. A countdown timer runs. No paid push needed — four weeks of buildup do the work.
in LinkedIn + X
Organic reposts carry the launch. The Nikhil Kamath post from last week is still live. Mid-tier finance creators who got early app access begin posting authentic reactions.
◙ OOH + PR
OOH updated — the 1% Club logo appears for the first time. Tagline: “Your journey to Financial Independence starts here.” ET, Mint, and Inc42 cover the launch. The billboard’s question finally has an answer.
Week 06
Aug 18–24
FIRE number becomes the organic hook
Products in focusRetention only. No new product this week.
◆ Instagram
Member stories continue. “What’s your number?” becomes a cultural moment across the ICP — people are sharing, comparing with friends, tagging others.
in LinkedIn + X
Launch-week momentum carries organically. This is the week the community becomes the campaign. No new paid push.
▶ YouTube
No new video. The Aug 15 launch video is still being discovered through search and shares.
◙ OOH + PR
No new OOH. Brand presence maintained through earned media — member sharing, press coverage, social proof.
Week 07
Sep 1–7
Insurance gets its moment — a protection story, not a product pitch
Products in focusInsurance via Pillow — cross-sold to existing members as a protection story.
▶ YouTube
Sharan records a direct video: “You’re building wealth. One hospital bill can undo all of it.” He walks through what happens to a family’s savings without health cover. Then introduces Pillow — free advisor call, no commission pressure. Books one on camera, live.
◆ Instagram
A mini-documentary — one real member, before and after their Pillow call. What they thought they had. What they actually had. What changed. Real person. Real numbers. Documentary format, not testimonial.
in LinkedIn + X
No paid push. The YouTube video is shared organically by members who recognise their own situation. Insurance anxiety is high-resonance for the 32–42 ICP.
◙ OOH + PR
No new OOH. The Pillow story plays best on screen — the emotion is in the numbers, not a billboard.
Week 08
Sep 22–28
Fixed Deposits — introduced as an emergency fund decision, not a banking product
Products in focusFixed Deposits — the FD marketplace framed as an emergency fund decision.
▶ YouTube
“Where should your emergency fund actually sit?” Sharan compares savings account vs FD vs liquid fund. Opens the app live — Suryoday SF Bank at 7.25% p.a. Concrete. Real numbers. Not a feature walkthrough — a financial decision made on camera.
◆ Instagram
A carousel: “Your bank is giving you 3.5%. Here’s what you’re leaving on the table.” Interactive story: enter your emergency fund amount, see exactly how much you’re losing. High share rate.
in LinkedIn + X
No paid amplification. Anyone with a savings account feels the loss when they see the numbers. Inherently shareable for the 32–42 segment.
◙ OOH + PR
No new OOH. The FD story is a numbers play — it works best where people can do the math themselves.
Week 09
Oct 13–19
Credit Cards — a 30-day AI experiment, not a card discovery feature
Products in focusCredit Cards — introduced as a spend optimisation tool via Aly.
▶ YouTube
Aly presents: “I let AI decide which credit card to swipe for every purchase for 30 days.” Real experiment. Real savings tracked. The card wallet feature shown in action — not explained, demonstrated.
◆ Instagram
Aly’s reel from the 30-day experiment. Specific numbers. “Month 1: ₹4,200. Month 2: ₹6,800. Month 3: ₹7,100.” Compounding optimisation, visualised as a graph.
in LinkedIn + X
Paid push by LinkedIn influencers for Aly’s posts. The Credit Cards feature is the one product requiring a paid amplification push — the ICP for it skews more professional and LinkedIn-native.
◙ OOH + PR
No new OOH. The Credit Card story is personal and digital — it needs a screen, not a billboard.
